A Notification of Presentation (NOP) waiver is an authorisation that removes the need to present goods before they move when using an Entry in Declarant’s Records (EIDR) authorisation.

NOP waivers can only be used for goods moving via EIDR which are:

  • standard goods - for example, goods that are not subject to controls
  • classified as not at risk of onward movement into the European Union (EU)
  • moving via direct movement from Great Britain to Northern Ireland

Using a NOP waiver in the Goods Vehicle Movement System (GVMS)

If you’re moving goods on behalf of a trader who has a NOP waiver, you can tell HMRC about this when you are creating a goods movement reference (GMR). You will not need to enter an Entry Summary Declaration (ENS), also known as a Safety and Security declaration.

How to get a NOP waiver

You need to apply online to be authorised.

You’ll need to:

  • hold a valid Authorised Economic Operator (AEO) authorisation - either Full Authorised Economic Operator (AEOF) or Authorised Economic Operator Customs Simplification (AEOC) authorisation
  • hold a valid SCDP-EIDR authorisation or UKIMS-EIDR authorisation
  • declare that the NOP waiver is being used in line with the conditions set out

You’ll need to sign in with your Government Gateway ID and password. If you do not have a user ID, you can create one when you first try to sign in.
